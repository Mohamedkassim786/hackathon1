## Common Symptoms and What They Mean

### Fever
A fever is a temporary rise in body temperature, usually a sign the body is fighting an infection.
- Low-grade fever: 99°F–100.4°F (37.2°C–38°C)
- Moderate fever: 100.4°F–102.2°F (38°C–39°C)
- High fever: Above 102.2°F (39°C)
- Seek emergency care: Above 104°F (40°C) in adults, or any fever in infant under 3 months.
Signs: Sweating, chills, headache, muscle aches, loss of appetite, dehydration, weakness.

### Headache
Headaches can be tension-type (most common), migraines, cluster headaches, or secondary (caused by another illness).
- Tension headache: dull, aching pressure around the head, tight feeling in neck and scalp.
- Migraine: throbbing on one side, sensitivity to light and sound, nausea, lasts 4–72 hours.
- Cluster headache: severe burning pain around one eye, happens in clusters/cycles.
Warning signs to see a doctor immediately: sudden severe "thunderclap" headache, headache after head injury, headache with stiff neck/fever/confusion.

### Cold (Common Cold)
Caused by rhinovirus. Usually mild and clears in 7–10 days.
Symptoms: Runny or stuffy nose, sore throat, cough, mild body aches, mild headache, sneezing, low-grade fever, generally feeling unwell.

### Cough
A cough is a reflex to clear the airway.
- Dry cough: often from viral infections, allergies, or acid reflux.
- Wet cough (productive): produces mucus, often from respiratory infections.
- Chronic cough (lasting >8 weeks): see a doctor.
- Cough with blood or severe breathing difficulty: seek emergency care immediately.

### Stomach Pain / Abdominal Pain
Common causes: Indigestion, gas, constipation, diarrhea, food poisoning, ulcers.
Seek doctor urgently if: severe sudden pain, pain with vomiting blood, rigid abdomen, pain after injury.

### Diarrhea
Loose, watery stools occurring more than 3 times a day.
Causes: viral/bacterial infection, food intolerance, medications.
Key risk: dehydration — drink plenty of clear fluids (ORS solution, water, coconut water).
See doctor if: diarrhea lasts more than 2 days, blood in stool, severe dehydration signs (no urination, sunken eyes, extreme thirst).

### Sore Throat
Causes: viral infection (most common), strep bacteria, allergies.
Symptoms: Pain/scratch when swallowing, red swollen tonsils, white patches (if bacterial), hoarse voice.

### Body Pain / Muscle Aches
Common with viral infections like flu and COVID-19.
Causes: Physical activity, dehydration, flu, fibromyalgia, vitamin D deficiency.

### Nausea and Vomiting
Common causes: Food poisoning, viral infection, motion sickness, pregnancy (morning sickness), medication side effects.
Hydrate with small sips. Avoid solid food until stable.

### Fatigue / Weakness
Persistent tiredness not relieved by rest can indicate anemia, thyroid problems, diabetes, or depression.
If fatigue is sudden and severe — seek medical attention.

## Basic Home Remedies

### For Fever
- Rest and stay hydrated (water, ORS, coconut water, soups).
- Sponging with lukewarm (not cold) water helps reduce temperature.
- Wear light breathable clothing.
- Paracetamol (acetaminophen) as directed for adults reduces fever and discomfort.
- Do NOT use aspirin in children (risk of Reye's syndrome).

### For Headache
- Rest in a quiet, dark room.
- Apply a cold or warm compress to the forehead or neck.
- Stay hydrated — dehydration is a major trigger.
- Over-the-counter options: Paracetamol, Ibuprofen (with food).
- Avoid screen time, loud noise, and strong smells.
- For migraines: sleep, darkness, and cold compress are key.

### For Cold and Runny Nose
- Rest and drink warm fluids (ginger tea, honey-lemon water, warm soups).
- Steam inhalation to relieve congestion.
- Saltwater gargle for sore throat (1/2 tsp salt in a glass of warm water).
- Honey (for adults and children >1 year) soothes the throat.
- Saline nasal drops help relieve nasal congestion.

### For Cough
- Honey and warm water or honey-lemon tea (effective and safe).
- Steam inhalation with a towel over the head.
- Warm fluids thin the mucus.
- Elevate head while sleeping to ease nighttime cough.
- Dry cough: Cough suppressants (Dextromethorphan-based syrups) with caution.
- Wet cough: Expectorants (Guaifenesin) help loosen mucus.

### For Sore Throat
- Gargle with warm salt water 3–4 times daily.
- Honey and ginger tea.
- Throat lozenges or sprays for temporary relief.
- Warm soups and cold ice pops can both help soothe.

### For Stomach Pain / Indigestion
- Eat bland foods (BRAT diet): Bananas, Rice, Applesauce, Toast.
- Avoid spicy, fatty, or acidic foods.
- Antacids for indigestion/heartburn.
- Peppermint or ginger tea for gas/bloating.
- Smaller, more frequent meals.

### For Diarrhea
- Oral Rehydration Solution (ORS) — critical to prevent dehydration.
- BRAT diet: Bananas, Rice, Applesauce, Toast.
- Avoid dairy, fatty, or high-fiber foods until recovered.
- Probiotics (yogurt with live cultures) help restore gut flora.

### For Nausea
- Small sips of water or ginger tea/ale.
- Ginger (in any form) is well evidence for nausea.
- Avoid strong smells.
- Eat bland, small portions.
- Rest with head slightly elevated.

### For Body Pain / Muscle Aches
- Rest the affected area.
- Warm or cold compress (ice for acute injury, heat for muscle soreness).
- Gentle stretching.
- Ibuprofen or Paracetamol for pain relief.
- Stay hydrated.

## First Aid Tips

### Cuts and Minor Wounds
1. Stop the bleeding: Apply gentle pressure with a clean cloth or bandage for 10–15 minutes.
2. Clean the wound: Rinse with clean running water. Avoid hydrogen peroxide on the wound (it can damage tissue).
3. Apply antibiotic ointment (e.g., Neosporin/Bacitracin) if available.
4. Cover with a sterile bandage.
5. Change bandage daily. Watch for signs of infection: redness, swelling, pus, warmth, fever.
6. See a doctor if: wound is deep, edges won't close, caused by a rusty object (tetanus risk), or sign of infection.

### Burns
- Minor (1st degree — red, no blister): Cool under cold running water for 10–20 minutes. Do NOT use ice, butter, or toothpaste.
- Cover with sterile non-stick dressing.
- Aloe vera gel can soothe minor burns.
- See a doctor: burns larger than palm size, burns on face/hands/genitals/joints, blistering (2nd degree), charred/whitened skin (3rd degree).

### Choking (Adult / Child > 1 year)
1. If person is coughing forcefully — encourage them to keep coughing.
2. If cannot cough/breathe: Stand behind them, give 5 firm back blows between shoulder blades.
3. Then 5 abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver): hands just above navel, sharp upward thrusts.
4. Alternate back blows and abdominal thrusts until object is dislodged or emergency services arrive.

### Choking (Infant < 1 year)
- 5 back blows, then 5 chest thrusts (NOT abdominal).
- Call emergency services immediately.

### Sprains and Strains
Follow R.I.C.E. method:
- **Rest**: Avoid using the injured area.
- **Ice**: Apply ice pack (wrapped in a cloth) for 15–20 min every 2–3 hours.
- **Compression**: Wrap with elastic bandage to reduce swelling.
- **Elevation**: Keep injured limb raised above heart level.
- See a doctor if severe pain, inability to bear weight, or suspected fracture.

### Fainting
1. Lay the person flat on their back. Elevate legs 12 inches if no suspected spinal injury.
2. Loosen tight clothing around neck/waist.
3. Do not give anything by mouth until fully conscious.
4. If not breathing — begin CPR and call emergency services.
5. See a doctor after fainting to find the cause.

### Nosebleed
1. Sit upright and lean slightly forward (not back).
2. Pinch the soft part of the nose firmly for 10–15 minutes without releasing.
3. Breathe through the mouth.
4. Do NOT tilt head back (blood may go down throat).
5. See a doctor if: bleeding doesn't stop in 20 minutes, caused by head injury, or very heavy.

### Allergic Reaction
- Mild: Antihistamines (Loratadine, Cetirizine) for hives/itching.
- Anaphylaxis (severe): Hives + swelling of throat + difficulty breathing — use Epinephrine auto-injector (EpiPen) if available and call emergency services IMMEDIATELY.

### Eye Injury / Foreign Object in Eye
- Do NOT rub the eye.
- Flush with clean water or saline for 15–20 minutes.
- Seek medical care if object is embedded or vision is affected.

### Bee Sting
1. Remove stinger by scraping sideways (do NOT use tweezers — squeezes more venom).
2. Wash with soap and water.
3. Apply cold pack.
4. Antihistamine for itching/swelling.
5. Seek emergency care if signs of allergic reaction (difficulty breathing, swelling of throat).

## Medicine Usage (Basic & Safe)

### Paracetamol (Acetaminophen) — e.g., Crocin, Tylenol, Dolo 650
- **Used for**: Fever, mild-to-moderate pain (headache, body ache, toothache).
- **Adult dose**: 500mg–1000mg every 4–6 hours. Maximum 4000mg per day.
- **Children**: Weight-based dosing — consult packaging or doctor.
- **Caution**: Do NOT exceed recommended dose. Avoid alcohol. Dangerous in overdose (liver damage). Do NOT combine multiple products containing paracetamol.
- **Safe during pregnancy**: Yes, at recommended doses.

### Ibuprofen — e.g., Brufen, Advil, Nurofen
- **Used for**: Fever, inflammation, pain (headache, muscle ache, menstrual cramps, minor arthritis).
- **Adult dose**: 200mg–400mg every 4–6 hours with food. Maximum 1200mg/day (OTC).
- **Caution**: Take with food to protect stomach. Avoid if: kidney problems, stomach ulcers, heart disease, pregnancy (3rd trimester). Not for children under 6 months.

### Antacids — e.g., Gelusil, Gaviscon, Mylanta
- **Used for**: Heartburn, acid reflux, indigestion, stomach upset.
- Take 30–60 minutes after meals or as needed.
- Do not take within 2 hours of other medications (reduces absorption).

### Oral Rehydration Salts (ORS) — e.g., Electral, WHO-ORS
- **Used for**: Dehydration from diarrhea, vomiting, fever, heat exposure.
- Mix one sachet in 1 liter of clean water. Sip slowly throughout the day.
- Safe for all ages. Essential for children with diarrhea.

### Antihistamines — e.g., Cetirizine (Zyrtec), Loratadine (Clarityn)
- **Used for**: Allergies, hay fever, hives, itching, runny nose.
- Cetirizine 10mg once daily; Loratadine 10mg once daily (non-drowsy).
- Older antihistamines (Chlorpheniramine/Benadryl) can cause drowsiness — avoid driving.

### Cough Syrup Types
- **Expectorant** (e.g., Guaifenesin/Mucinex): For wet productive cough — thins and loosens mucus.
- **Suppressant** (e.g., Dextromethorphan): For dry non-productive cough — reduces urge to cough.
- **Combination**: Many syrups combine both — read labels carefully.
- Children under 4: Do NOT use adult cough medicines. Consult a pediatrician.

### Saline Nasal Spray / Drops
- Safe for all ages including infants.
- Moisturizes nasal passages and relieves congestion.
- Can be used multiple times per day.

### Probiotics — e.g., Enterogermina, Lactobacillus-based supplements
- **Used for**: Restoring gut flora after antibiotic use, reducing diarrhea duration, general digestive health.
- Take with or after meals. Refrigerate if required.

### Topical Antiseptics — e.g., Povidone-Iodine (Betadine), Chlorhexidine
- **Used for**: Cleaning minor cuts and wounds.
- Apply to clean wound, let dry.
- Do not use extensively inside deep wounds.

## When to Seek Emergency Medical Care IMMEDIATELY

Call emergency services (ambulance/911/108) if you notice:
- Chest pain, pressure, or tightness (possible heart attack)
- Sudden numbness/weakness on one side of body or face (possible stroke)
- Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath at rest
- High fever above 104°F (40°C) unresponsive to medication
- Seizures or loss of consciousness
- Severe allergic reaction (throat swelling, difficulty breathing)
- Severe abdominal pain, vomiting blood
- Uncontrolled bleeding
- Signs of severe dehydration (no urine, sunken eyes, rapid heartbeat)
- Any symptom that is sudden, severe, or worsening rapidly

## General Health and Wellness Tips

- Drink 8–10 glasses of water daily.
- Eat a balanced diet rich in vegetables, fruits, whole grains, and lean protein.
- Sleep 7–9 hours per night.
- Exercise at least 30 minutes most days.
- Wash hands frequently with soap and water — the single most effective disease prevention step.
- Do not self-medicate with antibiotics — they only work for bacterial infections, not viruses.
- Keep a regular health check-up schedule even when feeling well.
- Manage stress through relaxation techniques, walking, meditation, or hobbies.
- Always read medication labels and complete the full prescribed course.
- Store medicines safely away from children and heat/moisture.
